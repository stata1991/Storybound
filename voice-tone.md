# Storybound — Brand Voice & Tone

---

## Brand Essence

**What Storybound is:** A quarterly storybook subscription where your child is the hero of a growing saga.

**What Storybound feels like:** The best children's librarian you've ever met — warm, imaginative, and deeply invested in your child specifically. Not generic. Not automated-feeling. Genuinely crafted.

**The emotional promise:** "Your child's story, told beautifully, four times a year."

---

## Voice Principles

### 1. Personal, not personalized
There's a difference between swapping a name into a template and actually knowing a child. Our copy should reflect that we know *this* child — their interests, their quirks, their world.

❌ "A personalized storybook for your child"
✅ "A story written around who your child actually is"

### 2. Warm, not saccharine
We love children and families. But we don't drip with sentiment. Confident warmth, not syrupy sweetness.

❌ "The most magical gift you'll ever give your precious little one ✨"
✅ "Four times a year, your child gets to be the hero. That's it. That's the whole thing."

### 3. Confident, not salesy
We know what we are. We don't oversell. We let the product speak.

❌ "The BEST personalized storybook subscription on the market!"
✅ "Most personalized books get read twice. These get kept."

### 4. Specific, not vague
Specificity builds trust. Vague promises sound like every other brand.

❌ "A unique experience tailored to your child"
✅ "If your daughter is obsessed with dinosaurs, the princess in the story wears a dinosaur crown. We don't override who she is."

### 5. Respectful of parents' time
Parents are busy. Get to the point. Respect their intelligence.

❌ "In today's fast-paced world, creating meaningful memories with your children has never been more important..."
✅ "Submit 3 photos. Answer 2 questions. We handle the rest."

## Copy Examples by Context

### Hero section (landing page)
> Most personalized storybooks change the name. We change the story.
> 
> Storybound is a quarterly storybook subscription where your child is the actual hero — not a template stand-in. Four times a year, we turn their photos and memories into a beautifully illustrated chapter they'll want to read again and again.

### How it works
> **Submit a memory drop.**
> Four times a year, we ask for 2-3 photos and 2 questions. What happened this season? What are they into right now? Takes 5 minutes.
> 
> **We write the chapter.**
> Our story team crafts a personalized adventure — with illustrations that actually look like your child.
> 
> **It arrives before their birthday.**
> Q4 is always the birthday book. It lands on their doorstep 2 weeks before the big day.

### Email subject lines (memory drop reminder)
- "It's time for [child's name]'s spring chapter 📖"
- "The summer memory window is open — 3 weeks left"
- "[Child's name]'s birthday book is next. Here's what we need."
- "We're about to write Episode 3. Missing your photos."

### Email subject lines (delivery)
- "[Child's name]'s autumn chapter is on its way"
- "Your birthday delivery is en route 🎂"
- "The summer chapter shipped. It's a good one."

### Retention copy (renewal)
> Year 2 is where the story gets good.
> 
> [Child's name] has three chapters behind them. Next year, the story remembers everything — the dragon from winter, the quest from summer, the secret from autumn. That's not something you can buy as a one-time book.

### Error / edge case copy
> We didn't receive photos this quarter. No worries — we'll continue [child's name]'s story using what we know about them. It won't be quite as personal, but it'll still be great. You'll have first right of refusal before we print.

## What We Never Say

- "Magical" (overused in children's products)
- "One-of-a-kind" (cliché)
- "Premium quality" (show, don't tell)
- "Seamless experience" (tech jargon, wrong register)
- "Bespoke" (too precious)
- Excessive exclamation points
