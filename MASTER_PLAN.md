# Storybound — Master Execution Plan

> *A quarterly storybook subscription where your child is the hero. Four chapters a year. One growing saga.*

---

## The Core Model

**What it is:** Parents subscribe annually. Every quarter, they submit 2-3 photos and answer 2 short questions about what happened that season. Storybound generates a personalized illustrated chapter book delivered to their door. Four books per year = one complete season of their child's story.

**Why quarterly (not annual, not monthly):**
- Annual: too much photo curation, parents forget, one failure point
- Monthly: economics don't work, stories become repetitive, operational chaos
- Quarterly: natural memory clusters (spring/summer/fall/birthday), low photo burden, 4 meaningful touchpoints per year, manageable operations

**The birthday anchor:** Q4 is always the birthday book — the chapter where the season culminates. The birthday is the emotional high point of the year. Three chapters build toward it.

### One-Time Book ($29)

One-time books use a standalone adventure — no story bible, no callbacks, no continuity. The parent completes a simplified child profile (name, age, interests, one photo) and receives a single illustrated book.

On the confirmation page, show upsell: *"Love it? Subscribe to continue their story from here — your one-time book becomes Chapter 1."*

If the parent converts to a subscription, the one-time book retroactively becomes Episode 1 of their first season, and a story bible is generated to continue the arc.

### Gift Subscription ($89/year)

Buyer pays $89 → receives a claim link → forwards to recipient → recipient completes child profile → story begins. Buyer does not fill in child details.

- Claim link expires after 90 days
- Recipient creates their own family account on claim
- Gift shows as "founding" pricing if purchased during founding window
- If unclaimed after 90 days, buyer is notified and offered a refund or extension

## The Quarterly Cycle

```
Q1 — Spring Chapter (March delivery)
  Memory window opens: January 15
  Memory window closes: February 10
  Print & ship: February 11–28
  Arrives: Early March

Q2 — Summer Chapter (June delivery)
  Memory window opens: April 15
  Memory window closes: May 10
  Print & ship: May 11–28
  Arrives: Early June

Q3 — Autumn Chapter (September delivery)
  Memory window opens: July 15
  Memory window closes: August 10
  Print & ship: August 11–28
  Arrives: Early September

Q4 — Birthday Chapter (2 weeks before birthday)
  Memory window opens: 8 weeks before birthday
  Memory window closes: 5 weeks before birthday
  Print & ship: 5–3 weeks before birthday
  Arrives: 1–2 weeks before birthday
```

*Note: Q1–Q3 are fixed seasonal windows. Q4 is birthday-relative and unique to each family.*

## Story Architecture

Each year is a **Season**. Each quarter is an **Episode**. Episodes are self-contained adventures (beginning, middle, end) but thread into a larger world.

```
Year 1: The Discovery Season
  Episode 1 — The Hidden Valley (spring adventure)
  Episode 2 — The Summer Storm (summer challenge)
  Episode 3 — The Autumn Secret (fall mystery)
  Episode 4 — The Birthday Quest (birthday culmination)

Year 2: The Courage Season
  (New world, familiar characters, raised stakes)
```

**Continuity mechanics:**
- Recurring sidekick introduced in Ep1, evolves each episode
- Child's real interests/fears woven into each episode
- Episode 4 always references moments from Eps 1–3 of that year
- Year 2 opens with a callback to Year 1's climax

## Phases

### Phase 0 — Proof of Product (Weeks 1–4)
**Goal:** Prove the AI + illustration pipeline produces books parents love
- Create 3 complete quarterly books manually (different ages, interests, families)
- Test the episodic story structure
- Test photo-to-illustration quality
- Go/no-go: Would you pay $109/year for these? (internal honest answer)

### Phase 1 — Concept Validation (Weeks 5–10)
**Goal:** Prove parents will pay before you build
- Landing page with waitlist + founding member offer
- 500 email signups
- 50 founding member conversions at $89
- Go/no-go: 50 paying families OR strong signal to adjust

### Phase 2 — Product Validation (Weeks 11–18)
**Goal:** Prove parents stay subscribed after receiving books
- Fulfill first quarter for 50 founding families
- Measure: Did the book arrive on time? Did the child love it? Would they renew?
- Go/no-go: >80% say they'd renew, NPS >50

### Phase 3 — Retention Validation (Months 5–12)
**Goal:** Prove quarterly model drives lower churn than annual
- Get 3 quarters of data on same families
- Measure photo submission rate, engagement, churn
- Go/no-go: <30% annual churn, >70% submit photos on time

### Phase 4 — Scale
- 1,000 families
- Operational automation
- Founder's Chest rollout

## The Founder's Chest

A premium physical artifact delivered to new subscribers — a keepsake box designed to hold all four quarterly books. Opens with a magnetic clasp. Child's name embossed on the lid.

**Why it matters:**
- Psychological commitment device — the box needs to be filled
- Shareable (parents show friends, creates organic word-of-mouth)
- Raises perceived value of the subscription
- Physical competitor moat — digital-only competitors cannot offer this

**When:** Introduced at Phase 4 (1,000+ families). Cost-prohibitive at smaller scale.
